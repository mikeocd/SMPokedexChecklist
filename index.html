The below checklist roughly follows Serebii's list of cells/cores. Consult their list if you need to see images.

  • Route 1, in your bedroom

  • Konikoni City, in Olivia's room, upstairs in her shop
  • Route 17, in disused police station full of Meowth
  • Ancient Poni Path, in the farmhouse

Melemele Island Cells

  • Route 1: Northwest between cliff and grass (Day)
  • Route 1: in area south accessibly by Tauros Charge south of Iki town. Go down the hill and It's under a tree. (Day)
  • Route 1: next to Kukui's house
  • Route 1: Trainer school, on cushion (Night)
  • Route 2: On the west side of the Motel
  • Route 3: Nestled next to cliff towards east side of route (Day)
  • Route 3: Follow the route until you cross the bridge, go down the hill and it's under a tree.
  • Hau'oli Cemetary: West side, in front of bush
  • Hau'oli City: Shopping district, upper floor of Ilima's house
  • Hau'oli City: Next to Pokemon Centre entrance (Night)
  • Iki Town: Behind man stood to the right-hand side of the path leading to the Ruins of Conflict (Night)
  • Kala'e bay: Follow the cliff to the mouth of the river. It's on the north side
  • Ruins of Conflict: right of the entrance to Tapu's den
  • Verdant Cavern: Next to one of the Yungoos nests from the trial

Akala Island Cells

  • Route 4: Just around the first bend, to the left
  • Route 5: On the path behind the Pokemon centre
  • Route 7: On the beach adjacent to Wela Mountain Park. Follow the cliff line north until it meets the edge of the beach
  • Route 8: To the right of the entrance to the temporary building. You met Colress on the road in front of it
  • Route 8: Next to the rock on the junction between Route 8 and Lush Jungle (Day)
  • Akala Outskirts: Next to the eastern entrance to Memorial Hill, on top of a small (jumpable) cliff
  • Diglett's cave: Next to the box with the nugget
  • Hano Beach: In the shade beneath a beach parasol
  • Hano Beach: Beneath the lifeguard tower (Day)
  • Heahea City: In the car park in front of the dimensional laboratory (Night)
  • Heahea City: In the western corner of the hotel, opposite the dancer
  • Konikoni City: Go through the large gate at the end of the city towards the peninsula with the Pikachu on. It's on the edge of the cliff (Night)
  • Lush Jungle: in the eastern quadrant of the jungle, next to the Machamp boulder
  • Paniola Ranch: On the farmstead itself, approximately north east of the Pokemon centre in the corner where some fences meet (Day)
  • Paniola Ranch: Once again on the farmstead, to the left of the first 'ramp' (assuming that you are coming from the Pokemon Centre) (Night)
  • Royal Avenue: To the right hand side of the Pokemon Centre, tucked into the corner where the building meets the fence (Night)
  • Royal Avenue: In the Thrifty Megamart Car Park, sat in an empty bay (Day)
  • Wela Volcano Park: At the peak of the Volcano

Ula'ula Island Cells

  • Route 10: Follow the cliffs on the right hand side of the road. It's sat next to a grove beneath the cliffs (Day)
  • Route 11: Sat on the south side of the road just before the transition to Route 12 (Night)
  • Route 12: Assuming you're coming from Route 11, after the first set of Mudsdale rocks, there's a small boulder on the edge of the peninsula. It's on the side south of that boulder.
  • Route 12: Again, assuming you're following this guide, go over the next set of Musdale rocks. The cell is just south of the punk trainers.
  • Route 13: To the right of a trailer, directly south of the oasis
  • Route 13: It's on the outside of the motel, to the left between the final chalet room and the cliffs (Night)
  • Route 14: Follow the shore to the east. It's at the most northern point, where the shore meets the cliffs
  • Route 14: It's directly south of the entrance from Tapu Village, to the left of the old man beneath where the road is out (Night)
  • Route 15: Go west from Aether House, down on to the beach. Then go south; the cell is in the shade beneath the cliffs
  • Route 16: The Pokemon Centre is on a platform. It's on the left hand side of that platform (Day)
  • Route 17: Follow the cliffs with all the one-way jumping spots to the top. Then make your way down via the jumping spots, and take the rightmost path on the first place the road forks. The cell is there
  • Route 17: Head north from the disused police station. The cell is next to the first corner of Po Town's surrounding wall.
  • Aether House: In the kids' room, to the far left (Day)
  • Blush Mountain: To the right of the entrance of the Geothermal Plant
  • Haina Desert: There's a balding man facing a small grassy patch. It's on the right of said patch
  • Hokulani Observatory: Go down the leftmost corridor. It's in there (Night)
  • Malie City Outer Cape: Go to the recycling plant north of the city. It's next to the vehicles on the right of the plant (Day)
  • Malie City: Between two buses (Day)
  • Malie City: In the community centre, down the corridor to the right, at the far end past the child (Night)
  • Malie Garden: Go as far north west as possibe. It's next to the canal
  • Mount Lanakila: Leave pokemon centre, go west. It's due south of the entrance to the Pokemon league (Day)
  • Mount Lanakila: On the lower part of the mountain, it's next to the rock with the escape rope
  • Po Town: Next to a flatbed truck in the northwest part of the town (Night)
  • Po Town: Next to a flatbed truck in the northeast part of the town (Day)
  • Ruins of Abundance: To the left of the entrance to the ruins
  • Secluded Shore: Follow the cliffs to the west. It's where the edge of the beach meets the cliffs (Day)
  • Secluded Shore: Essentially the same as the above, but follow the cliffs to the north east instead (Night)
  • Tapu Village: Leave the Pokemon centre. It's immediately south, where the gap in the fence is
  • Ula'ula Meadow: Enter from the south, and follow the boardwalk north. There's a plank jutting out on the right-hand side; the Cell is there.
  • Ula'ula Meadow: On the path towards the Lake of the Moone.

Poni Island Cells

  • Ancient Poni Path: To the left of the path leading North, out of Ancient Poni Path towards Poni Valley, tucked in the corner where two walls meet (Day)
  • Ancient Poni Path: Behind the well on the farmstead (Day)
  • Ancient Poni Path: To the right of the road leading towards Ancient Poni Path. There's a statue, the same as those dotted throughout the Valley; the cell is at the statue's foot (Night)
  • Poni Breaker Coast: Shortly after you enter the area from the west, there's a geyser with a small path surrounding it. It's on that path (Day)
  • Poni Breaker Coast: Next to the entrance to the ruins at the far end of the path (Night)
  • Poni Coast: In a path between two rocks; it's easily visible
  • Poni Coast: On the eastern peninsula, between a rock and the sea. Again, it's easily visible
  • Poni Gauntlet: Assuming you enter from the south, take the first bridge on the east to the small island with the Veteran and trainer tips sign on it. It's to the right of the sign and the trainer, just overlooking the sea
  • Poni Gauntlet: Enter from the north, then go south onto the bridge leading towards the battle tree. It's on the bridge

FAQ

What is this and who made it?

This is a Pokedex tracker for the Alola regional Pokedex made by Michael O'Connell-Davidson. It is a fork of (and was inspired by) the amazing Dark Souls 3 cheat sheet. For full credits, please see readme here.

How are Pokemon grouped?

Wild Pokemon are Pokemon that are caught (or derived from wild pokemon, i.e. Snubbull). I may change this to filter out evolutions in future.

Version exclusives are self-explanatory. Legendary / gift pokemon are 'one-ofs', such as Porygon or Type:Null.

Where did the information come from?

Serebii, Eurogamer, the Pokecommunity Daily, and Bulbapedia.

How do I read locations? It's not clear in some cases.

I tried to be as unambiguous or standard as possible, but some Pokemon are a pain in the ass. Semicolons are designed to separate alternate locations / methods. If something like 'night:' comes before a semicolon but not after, it can be done at any time of day. Name of Pokemon followed by SOS means that it's a result of that pokemon calling for help.

Why doesn't it give an exhaustive list of locations?

Some Pokemon appear in too many places to make this worthwhile. I tried to just pick the 'easiest' location in all cases, such as those near fly spots. I like Poni Island, as the stuff there is high level so if it's not fully evolved it's not hard to push it over the level threshhold. I didn't give locations for Ultra Beasts because the location alternates, so you should follow the quest for those.

I've noticed a mistake / have suggestions!

Oh no! Tweet me and I'll fix it. Alternatively, submit an issue above or fork and submit a pull request.

How is my progress saved?

HTML5 Browser storage - so don't clear your cookies / cache or you may lose your progress. It does mean that if I add new features you won't be able to see them WITHOUT clearing your cache, however.

Any other stuff worth saying?
